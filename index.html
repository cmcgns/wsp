Blood Earth Exhibition

Launch: Friday July 20, 7 p.m
Location: at Siddhartha Hall, Goethe-Institut/ Max Mueller Bhavan
Open: July 21 - July 28, 10 a.m to 7 p.m., all days except Sundays and public holidays

Word Sound Power's second project, Blood Earth, commenced in the Khonda tribal village of Kucheipadar in South Orissa. During the mid-1990s, large deposits of Bauxite, the mineral from which aluminum is made, were discovered in the area. Soon after, the district became a conflict zone where a vibrant people's movement blossomed with songs serving as a uniting force. With a mobile music studio, Word Sound Power joined local singers and musicians in Kucheipadar for a new type of collaboration.

The result is a cross genre mash up, combining revolutionary songs in Oriya and Kui tribal lan-guage with dub poetry by Delhi Sultanate and electronic music composed by Chris McGuinness. The exhibition is comprised of original music, soundscapes and video loops, as well as a film by Kush Badhwar which documents the collaborations. Blood Earth explores the relationship between music, struggle, and cultural responses to violence via word and sound.
